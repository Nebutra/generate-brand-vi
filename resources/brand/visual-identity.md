# generate-brand-kit Visual Identity

## Core Idea

**Parts in, brand out.** A brand kit is literally a kit: standardized parts
— mark, palette, type, asset slots — that assemble into one identity that
stays consistent across every surface. The mark shows that assembly in
progress: a square built from four distinct geometric part-tiles, with one
tile still snapping into place. The IP is abstracted from the business
scenario (kit assembly), not from any process metaphor.

One-sentence buyer test: "the tool that assembles your brand from parts
into a finished, consistent kit."

## Name and Voice

- English name: generate-brand-kit
- Short label: Brand Kit
- Tagline: parts in, brand out.
- Tone: engineering-first, calm, precise — a pipeline tool, not a design
  agency landing page. Voice stays fixed; tone may warm up in community
  copy (Show your kit) and tighten in reference docs.

## Visual System

- **Shape grammar:** a square mark assembled from four 2×2 quadrant tiles,
  each a *different* geometric part — quarter-disc, diagonal half-square
  triangle, dot-in-square, solid block. The bottom-right solid block is
  offset outward with a visible snap gap: the kit mid-assembly. One
  silhouette (square), one signal (the detached tile).
  Distinctiveness guard: four different part-shapes + the offset tile keep
  it from reading as Microsoft's four-pane flag or generic Metro tiles.
- **Materials:** silkscreen ink on paper, die-cut card, drafting-table
  precision. Flat solid color, crisp edges, subtle paper grain allowed in
  scene imagery only — never in the mark.
- **Palette (60/30/10, print-native, no gradients):**
  - Graphite `#1A1917` — warm near-black; dark surfaces, dark-mode tile
  - Paper `#FAF7F0` — warm off-white; light surfaces
  - Vermilion `#E4572E` — primary accent; the "tool" color
  - Cobalt `#274690` — secondary accent
  - Verified text pairs: Graphite-on-Paper 16.0:1; Paper-on-Graphite 16.0:1;
    Paper-on-Cobalt 9.4:1; Graphite-on-Vermilion 4.6:1. Vermilion-on-Paper
    (3.4:1) is for large graphics only, never body text.
- **Typography:** repo surfaces use GitHub-default rendering. Composed text
  in exported images: a geometric sans for headings with monospace spec
  labels (micrographics register), set after generation.
- **Imagery:** flat print-craft scenes — parts, die-cuts, registration-desk
  order; low visual noise where text or badges sit. No neon glow, no
  liquid, no glass.
- **Motion (latent):** the mark's native animation is assembly — tiles
  slide in and the gap closes. Use only where the surface calls for it.

## Negative Constraints

- No four-identical-color-squares composition (Microsoft flag reading).
- No droplet, water, or crystal imagery (retired first-generation brand).
- No AI-slop signature: purple-blue gradients, gradient orbs, glassmorphism,
  sparkle icons, fake 3D depth or bevels.
- No mascot, no generated text in rasters, no watermark.
- No rejected exploration assets under `approved/` or `processed/`.
