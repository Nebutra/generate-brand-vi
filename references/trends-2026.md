# Brand VI Aesthetic Trends (2025–2026)

Consult this during VI distillation (workflow step 3) to position a new
identity relative to the current landscape. Trends are inputs, not orders:
per Romaniuk, distinctive assets are built by consistency over years, so
pick trend alignment deliberately and expect it to age.
Sources verified 2026-07; re-check annually against the resources below.

## How to use trend reports

Treat named trends as a vocabulary of formal operations, not a menu of brand
concepts. `Crack`, `Open Axis`, `Echo Arcs`, `Phasers`, `Roll Over`, `Slash Bar`
and similar labels describe transformations such as interruption, retained gap,
repetition, sequencing, folding and directional cutting. Extract the operation,
then test whether it expresses a project-specific relationship. Never choose a
trend because its example logos look current, and never combine several trend
signatures into one mark merely to appear contemporary.

## Macro-currents

- **Motion is the 2026 macro-trend.** LogoLounge's 2026 report (30,000+
  logos, Bill Gardner) frames marks as fluid, dimensional, reactive, and
  system-driven. Motion-first identities are becoming baseline deliverables
  (Threerooms #1 shift; Pentagram's Cytora; Stripe/Linear/Vercel treat
  animated logo states as core brand). Quality bar: practitioners reject
  "motion for motion's sake."
- **Machine vs maker.** AI-slick output triggered a craft revival: Adobe's
  2026 Design Trends Report says 78% of designers intentionally add human
  imperfection (wobbly lines, irregular shapes, naive art). The market
  rewards AI-assisted work and punishes visibly pure-AI output.
- **Anti-blanding.** Creative Boom 2026: "the enemy of a good brand is no
  longer a bad brand, but one that is just okay." Weird, curated maximalism
  (Eternal Research, Fhirst, Clue Perfumery) answers sanitized sameness.
  Loewy's MAYA (Most Advanced Yet Acceptable) is the calibration dial.
- **Evolution over revolution.** 2025's respected rebrands were system
  refinements (Koto's Amazon, Walmart). Cracker Barrel's heritage-erasing
  reveal cost ~$100M in market cap and was reversed. For migrations:
  respect existing equity; audit what is distinctive before replacing it.

## Named rising aesthetics

| Trend | Signature | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Micrographics | tiny technical/blueprint details blown up as hero graphics | Astrae Studio for Nike; Uncommon billboards |
| Warning Low Ink | photocopier grain, dissolving marks | How&How's Big Cartel; Sonata Electronica |
| Blotch | fluid, melted, marker-bleed logos | Skims wordmark; Chantelle Pulp shape-shifting logotype |
| Visual Index | numbered specimen-style cut-out collections | Wonderhood's Parish Primary School |
| Pick-and-Mix | ransom-note scavenged letterforms | Trobar identity; Tapeface |
| Type-led identity | bespoke expressive letterforms as the hero asset; high-contrast serifs, friction type, variable fonts | Monotype Re:Vision themes |
| Dynamic/generative systems | rule-based living logos, parametric variation | Eventbrite "The Path" (BUCK); MIT Media Lab lineage |
| Soft-glow gradients | smoky ambient-light transitions replacing neon | Threerooms 2026 |
| Tactile imperfection | grain overlays, brush strokes, hand-hewn texture | How&How's Jupi (anti-slop AI branding) |
| Matured pixel revival | pixels as digital-physical metaphor, not 8-bit pastiche | bitmap type marks (Neue Pixel Grotesk) |

## Falling / fatigued (avoid unless deliberately subverted)

Glassmorphism and liquid-glass UI styling; gradients-on-everything; bento
grids; extended Y2K nostalgia; lazy minimalism AND lazy maximalism; Canva
template aesthetics; four-pointed AI sparkle (used by 7 of the top 10
software companies by 2024 — now a cliché); purple-blue gradient orbs;
splashy big-reveal rebrands.
